# AstroKobi — Newsletter Growth + Sponsorship Plan

A concrete, do-this-next plan for turning the auto-blog into an audience asset and
then a revenue line. Written against what already exists in this repo: a
Buttondown-backed signup form (`src/components/SubscribeForm.tsx` → `/api/subscribe`),
a weekly digest workflow (`.github/workflows/newsletter.yml` → `scripts/newsletter-digest.ts`),
AdSense slots, and the new `<GearBox>` affiliate components.

> **One-line strategy:** the hourly engine already manufactures *reach*; the
> newsletter converts reach into a *direct relationship*; sponsorship and
> affiliate convert the relationship into *revenue*. Build them in that order.

---

## Part 1 — The funnel as it stands today

| Stage | Mechanism in repo | Gap to close |
|-------|-------------------|--------------|
| Acquire | Hourly SEO posts + RSS + social syndication | No email capture above the fold |
| Capture | `SubscribeForm` (footer only) | Single placement, no incentive to subscribe |
| Activate | Buttondown welcome email | No welcome sequence / lead magnet delivery |
| Retain | Weekly digest (draft, manual send) | Digest is a link dump, not a *newsletter* |
| Monetize | AdSense + (new) affiliate `<GearBox>` | No sponsorship slot, no rate card |

The fastest wins are all in **Capture** and **Retain** — the traffic engine is
already running.

## Part 2 — Newsletter growth plan

### 2.1 Capture: get the form in front of readers

The form currently lives only in the footer. Add three placements (no new
infra — reuse `<SubscribeForm/>`):

1. **Inline mid-article CTA** — drop a subscribe block after the `## Why it matters`
   section in the article template (`src/app/blog/[slug]/page.tsx`), mirroring how
   the in-article `AdSlot` is already injected. This is the highest-converting spot.
2. **End-of-article CTA** — a "Get the weekly cosmos digest" block above the
   "Keep reading" related-posts list.
3. **Exit-intent / sticky bar** — a dismissible top bar on desktop. Optional;
   ship 1 and 2 first.

**Lead magnet (the single highest-leverage addition).** People subscribe for a
*thing*, not for "updates." For a space niche, the proven magnets are:

- **"The 2026 Sky Calendar"** — every meteor shower, eclipse, supermoon, and
  planetary conjunction with dates and viewing tips. One evergreen PDF, refreshed
  yearly. Cheap to produce, extremely on-niche, and pairs perfectly with the new
  affiliate gear boxes (binoculars, telescopes).
- Alternative: **"Backyard Astronomy Starter Kit"** — a one-page guide to your
  first telescope/binoculars + first 10 objects to find. Doubles as an affiliate
  surface.

Deliver it via Buttondown's automated welcome email. Set the form's value prop to
the magnet, not the blog ("Get the free 2026 Sky Calendar + a weekly digest").

### 2.2 Activate: a real welcome sequence

In Buttondown, build a 3-email automation that fires on subscribe:

1. **#0 (instant):** deliver the lead magnet. Set expectations ("one email a
   week, Sunday morning"). Ask one question (reply-to → boosts deliverability).
2. **#1 (day 2):** your 3 best evergreen explainers (e.g. Fermi paradox, black
   holes, exoplanets). Establishes the voice.
3. **#2 (day 5):** "What kind of space reader are you?" — a one-click poll
   (missions / deep astrophysics / stargazing how-to). Use the answer to segment.

### 2.3 Retain: upgrade the digest from link-dump to newsletter

`scripts/newsletter-digest.ts` + `src/lib/newsletter/digest.ts` currently emit a
title + description list. Make it feel handwritten without adding human labor:

- **Add a 2–3 sentence "From the control room" intro** generated by the same LLM
  the engine already uses — feed it the week's post titles and ask for a warm,
  curatorial lead. This is the single change that makes the digest read like a
  newsletter instead of an RSS dump.
- **Lead with one "story of the week,"** then the rest as a scannable list.
- **One "gear we'd point a friend to" line** at the bottom → an affiliate
  `<GearPick>`-style link. Native, useful, and the first newsletter revenue.
- Keep the current draft-not-auto-send flow — a human glance per week is the only
  quality gate and it costs 60 seconds.

### 2.4 Growth loops (compounding, near-zero cost)

- **SEO is the primary engine** — it's already running hourly. Make sure every
  post links the newsletter (placements above) so organic traffic converts.
- **Syndication footer** — the Bluesky/Mastodon/DEV cross-posts should end with
  "☄️ Weekly cosmos digest → astrokobi.com." One-line change in the syndication
  templates.
- **Referral ask** — Buttondown supports a referral/share link; add "forward this
  to a fellow stargazer" to the digest footer.
- **Reddit-native value** — the engine already pulls from r/space, r/astronomy,
  etc. Manually share genuinely good explainers (not every post) where the rules
  allow; the sky-calendar magnet is highly shareable there.

### 2.5 Targets (realistic for a near-zero-cost solo site)

| Milestone | Subscribers | What it unlocks |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Month 1–2 | 0 → 250 | Magnet + inline CTA live; baseline open rate |
| Month 3–6 | 250 → 1,500 | Welcome sequence + segmented digest |
| Month 6–12 | 1,500 → 5,000 | **Sponsorship-viable** (see Part 3) |

Track only what matters: **subscriber count, open rate (target 40%+ for a
content newsletter), and click rate (target 5%+).** Buttondown reports all three.

## Part 3 — Sponsorship plan

A space/astronomy newsletter has unusually good sponsor-fit: telescope and optics
brands, astrophotography software, STEM toys/education, planetariums and science
museums, space-tourism and model brands, and science-book publishers.

### 3.1 When to start

Sponsorship pricing follows a rough **$20–$40 per 1,000 opens (CPM on opens)**
for a niche newsletter. Don't sell sponsorship below ~**2,000 engaged
subscribers** — before that, affiliate (`<GearBox>`) is the better monetization
and you avoid burning sponsor relationships on tiny sends.

### 3.2 Inventory (what you actually sell)

1. **Primary newsletter slot** — one sponsor per weekly send, a 2–3 sentence
   "Presented by" block below the intro. One sponsor/week keeps it clean.
2. **Classified / secondary** — a single one-line text ad lower down.
3. **Site sponsorship** — a sponsored `<GearBox>`-style unit or a "Sponsored by"
   line in the article template, sold per-month (complements AdSense, doesn't
   replace it).
4. **Dedicated send** — occasional, premium, clearly labeled. Use sparingly.

### 3.3 Rate card (starting point — adjust to real open numbers)

| Product | Audience assumption | Price |
|---------|--------------------|-------|
| Weekly primary slot | 2,000 opens | $40–$80 / send |
| Weekly primary slot | 5,000 opens | $100–$200 / send |
| 4-week package | — | 10–15% discount |
| Site `<GearBox>` sponsor unit | site traffic | $150–$400 / month |
| Dedicated send | — | 2–3× the weekly slot |

Always quote **per-send** and offer a discounted first run ("intro rate, one
send, no commitment") to lower the barrier.

### 3.4 Outreach playbook

- **Affiliate-first as proof.** The new `<GearBox>` units double as a sponsor
  pipeline: a telescope brand whose product converts via your affiliate link is
  the warmest possible sponsorship lead. Track which gear links click.
- **Target list:** telescope/optics retailers (Celestron, Orion, High Point
  Scientific), astrophotography software (PixInsight, Astro Pixel Processor),
  STEM/education brands, science publishers, planetarium apps (Stellarium,
  SkySafari), space-model and merch brands.
- **The pitch email** (keep it 5 sentences): who you are, the audience (size +
  niche + engagement rate), one screenshot of the newsletter, the intro rate, a
  link to the media kit. Ask for a 1-send trial.
- **Media kit** — a single page: audience size, open/click rates, subscriber
  description, sample issue, slot options, rate card, contact. Host it at
  `/sponsor` (a new static route) so the pitch is just a link.
- **Sponsorship marketplaces** — once at scale, list on Paved, Swapstack, or
  Hecto to get inbound instead of only outbound.

### 3.5 Guardrails

- **Always label** sponsored content ("Presented by" / "Sponsored"). The
  `<GearBox>` already ships `rel="sponsored nofollow"` + an FTC disclosure — hold
  the same standard for paid placements.
- **One sponsor per send**, relevant to space/astronomy only. Relevance is the
  whole moat; protect it.
- **Never let a sponsor write the editorial.** Sponsor block is sponsor copy;
  the digest stays independent.
