This year also brings bittersweet news for the global astronomy community: 2026 will be the final year of his renowned Astronomical Calendar. For decades, Ottewell's unique blend of scientific precision and artistic illustration has served as an indispensable guide for sky-watchers and educators, making complex celestial mechanics accessible and beautiful.

## What happened
Guy Ottewell, a figure revered across the astronomy world, celebrated his 90th birthday on July 4, 2026. This milestone arrives as his highly influential Astronomical Calendar is set to publish its final edition this year. First launched in 1974, the calendar quickly became a staple, offering detailed charts and hand-illustrated guides that made celestial events understandable to a broad audience.

The Astronomical Calendar enjoyed a long print run until 2016, reaching thousands of enthusiasts in over 100 countries. After a brief hiatus, it returned in 2023 in both print and electronic formats, much to the delight of its dedicated following. Ottewell's journey into astronomy was unconventional, stemming from open-air camping and a teaching stint at a Navajo school where he first used a telescope. He later taught himself trigonometry and computer programming to create the calendar, initially published by Furman University's Physics Department.

## Why it matters
Ottewell's Astronomical Calendar has been more than just a datebook; it has been a vital educational tool, widely employed by astronomy educators to teach about the night sky. Its distinctive visual approach demystified complex astronomical phenomena, fostering a deeper appreciation for space among millions. The calendar's conclusion represents the end of an era for a unique, hand-crafted resource in an increasingly digital world, highlighting the enduring value of personalized scientific communication.

His work transcends mere data presentation, integrating artistic talent with scientific rigor. This unique approach allowed Ottewell to bridge the gap between academic astronomy and public engagement, inspiring generations of amateur astronomers and professionals alike. The cessation of the calendar marks the retirement of a publication that embodied a singular vision, leaving a void that will be challenging to fill with resources of comparable charm and clarity.
